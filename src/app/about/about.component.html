— My Story
Sana Burney is a master's in Fine art from the University of Central Lancashire, UK. She is currently on the Indus Valley School of Art & Architecture faculty and has taught for over 12 years. She has presented her research papers on art and design in Turkey, the USA and Pakistan.

She has done gigantic public art projects in Karachi and Larkana. Karachi's public art project of 'FTC Bridge Beautification' is an example of beauty, patriotism, and nationalism. She has designed murals and light works and imagined and led projects with distinctive styles.

Additionally, she has worked with Karachi Biennale and painted two huge reels for the 'Reel on Hai' project. Reels were launched in March 2017 and placed in Safari Park. In 2021, she participated in Jade Montserrat's project of mural drawing at the University of Central Lancashire, Preston, UK. Besides, she has exhibited at several art galleries in Pakistan.

Burney's art practice revolves around the themes of colonialism, post-colonial societies, racialized inequality, institutionalized racism, subordination, dependency, belonging and not belonging, capitalism, and the ideas of feminism. Subjects of hybrid identities and identity crises are prominent themes in her practice. Besides, she is also interested in sociology and psychoanalytic perspectives. Her practice originates from twentieth-century modern collage styles and evolves into a diverse range of alternative techniques, assemblage and found objects combining contemporary influences. It also encompasses traditional types of printmaking, entailing new methods and experiments.
